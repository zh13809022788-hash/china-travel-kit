China Tourist Visa (L Visa) Application Guide 2026: Step by Step for US, UK, Canada, Australia & More

Complete 2026 guide to applying for a Chinese tourist visa — who needs one, documents required, application process, fees, processing times at Chinese visa centers worldwide, and how to avoid common rejection reasons.

Introduction

If you are a passport holder from the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, or a number of other countries who does not qualify for China’s visa-free programs, you will need to apply for a tourist visa before you travel. And unlike the old days, China’s visa application process has moved almost entirely to outsourced Visa Application Service Centers, with an online form system that has strict formatting rules and no margin for error.

This guide covers exactly what you need to apply for a China tourist visa in 2026, from the forms and documents you need to the step-by-step process at the visa center. It includes specific information for each major English-speaking country and the most common reasons applications get rejected.

Last reviewed: July 20, 2026. Visa fees and processing times change; confirm current details on your local visa center website before applying.

Who Needs a Tourist Visa?

Before you go through the application process, confirm whether you actually need a visa. There are two ways to enter China without one in 2026:

  • 30-day unilateral visa-free entry: covers a growing list of countries for tourism, business, and family visits.
  • 240-hour transit exemption: allows eligible travelers passing through China to a third country to stay up to 10 days without a visa.

If neither applies to your nationality and itinerary, you need a regular visa. This applies to most travelers from the United States, the United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, India, and the Philippines.

The L Visa: What It Is and What It Allows

The L visa is China’s standard tourist visa. It allows you to enter mainland China for tourism, sightseeing, and short-term leisure, stay for up to 30 or 60 days per entry, and travel freely within mainland China (excluding Tibet without a separate permit). You can apply for a single-entry, double-entry, or multiple-entry visa.

The L visa does not permit paid work, study, journalism, or permanent residence.

Documents You Need

1. Passport

Valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended departure from China, with at least 2 blank visa pages, not damaged, and issued within the last 10 years.

2. Visa Application Form (COVA)

Completed online at cova.cs.mfa.gov.cn. Every field must be filled. Empty fields are treated as errors. Print single-sided on A4 paper. Your signature must match your passport.

3. Passport Photo

One recent photo, white background, 33mm x 48mm, no glasses, full face visible. Taken within the last 6 months.

4. Flight Itinerary

Round-trip flight booking or confirmed itinerary. You do not need to purchase non-refundable tickets before the visa is approved. A reservation hold from your airline or travel agent is sufficient.

5. Hotel Bookings or Travel Itinerary

Hotel confirmations for your entire stay. If staying with friends or family, you need a formal invitation letter with the host’s ID copy and residential address.

6. Proof of Sufficient Funds

Bank statements from the last 3 to 6 months showing regular income and a balance sufficient for your trip. As a guideline, expect to show at least $100-150 per day of your intended stay.

7. Previous Chinese Visas

Copies of any previous Chinese visas you have held. This helps demonstrate your travel history and can simplify approval.

8. Proof of Residence (if applicable)

If applying outside your home country, include your valid visa or residence permit showing legal status extending at least 3 months beyond your application date.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Determine Where to Apply

You must apply at the Chinese Visa Application Service Center that covers your legal residence. Major locations include:

  • United States: Washington DC, New York, Chicago, San Francisco, Los Angeles
  • United Kingdom: London, Manchester, Edinburgh, Belfast
  • Canada: Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary, Montreal, Ottawa
  • Australia: Canberra, Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide

The center is determined by your residential address, not where you are visiting.

Step 2: Complete the Online Application Form

Go to cova.cs.mfa.gov.cn and create an account. Complete the form with your exact passport details. Critical tips:

  • Your name must match your passport exactly, including middle names
  • Your passport number, issue date, and expiration date must match the machine-readable zone
  • Complete every field — empty fields are treated as errors
  • Do not apply more than 3 months before your intended travel date

Save the completed form as a PDF, print it single-sided, and sign the designated sections.

Step 3: Gather Your Documents

Organize your documents in this order:

  1. Completed and signed COVA form
  2. Passport
  3. Photocopy of passport bio page
  4. Passport photo
  5. Flight itinerary
  6. Hotel bookings
  7. Bank statements
  8. Supporting documents (invitation letter, previous visa copies, proof of residence)

Step 4: Submit Your Application

Visit your designated visa center. Most accept walk-in applications, though some require an online appointment. At the center you will have your documents reviewed, pay the visa fee plus service fee, provide fingerprints (if you are aged 14 to 70), and receive a tracking receipt. The process takes 15 to 30 minutes.

Step 5: Wait for Processing

Standard processing takes 4 to 5 business days. Express service (2 to 3 business days) and rush service (1 business day) are available for additional fees in most locations. Track your application online using the number on your receipt.

Step 6: Collect Your Passport

Return to the center to collect your passport with the visa affixed. Check that your name, passport number, photo, validity dates, number of entries, and duration of stay are all correct before leaving.

Fees

Visa fees vary by nationality due to reciprocity agreements. The total cost includes the consular visa fee and the visa center service fee. As rough estimates:

  • US citizens: approx. $185 total
  • UK citizens: approx. 150-170 GBP total
  • Canadian citizens: approx. 120-160 CAD total
  • Australian citizens: approx. 150-180 AUD total
  • Most EU citizens: approx. 50-100 EUR total

Express and rush services add additional fees. Check your local visa center website for the exact current fee schedule.

Common Reasons for Visa Rejection

Incorrect Application Form

The COVA form has strict validation. Name must match passport exactly, every field must be completed, and the form must be printed single-sided.

Insufficient Financial Proof

Bank statement balance too low, no regular income pattern, statement older than 30 days, or statement not in your name. Show at least $100-150 per day of your stay.

Weak Ties to Home Country

No stable employment, no family ties, or previous overstays in any country. The visa officer needs to be confident you will leave China after your visit.

Incomplete Travel Itinerary

Hotel bookings not covering your full stay, itinerary gaps, or a route that does not make geographic sense. Every night needs accommodation.

Applying at the Wrong Time

More than 3 months before travel (not accepted), or less than 2 weeks before departure (too late for standard processing).

Passport Issues

Passport expiring within 6 months, fewer than 2 blank visa pages, or a damaged passport. These are grounds for immediate rejection.

Frequently Asked Questions

How early should I apply?

Apply between 1 and 3 months before your travel date. Applications more than 3 months ahead are typically not accepted. Applications less than 2 weeks before departure are risky unless you pay for express service.

Can I extend my L visa once inside China?

Yes. Apply at the local Public Security Bureau Exit-Entry Administration office in the city where you are staying. You need a valid reason, proof of funds, and a confirmed itinerary. Extensions are typically granted for 30 days. Apply at least 7 days before your current visa expires.

What happens if I overstay?

Overstaying results in fines of approximately 500 RMB per day (up to 10,000 RMB maximum), possible questioning by immigration, and in serious cases a re-entry ban of 1 to 10 years. Apply for an extension before your visa expires.

How long is a Chinese tourist visa valid for?

The validity period (the window to use the visa) varies by nationality. US citizens can receive 10-year validity. Other nationalities typically receive 6 months to 2 years. The duration of each stay is usually 30 or 60 days.

Summary

Applying for a China tourist visa in 2026 is a straightforward but detail-sensitive process. Prepare a passport with 6+ months validity, complete the COVA form online, gather flight and hotel bookings plus bank statements, and submit in person at your local visa center. Standard processing takes 4 to 5 business days. The most common rejection reasons are all avoidable with careful preparation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a Chinese tourist visa in 2026?

If your passport is not covered by China's 30-day unilateral visa-free entry and your itinerary does not qualify for the 240-hour transit exemption, you need a regular visa. Most travelers from the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, Australia, New Zealand, and many European countries whose passports are not yet on the unilateral-free list fall into this category. Check your nationality against the current visa-free list before applying.

What is an L visa and what does it allow?

The L visa is China's standard tourist visa. It typically allows a single entry or multiple entries, with stays of up to 30 or 60 days per entry depending on what the visa office grants. With an L visa you can travel for tourism, sightseeing, and short-term leisure activities anywhere in mainland China. It does not permit work, study, journalism, or long-term residence.

What documents do I need for a China tourist visa application?

The standard requirements are: a passport valid for at least 6 months with at least 2 blank visa pages, a completed visa application form (COVA form), a recent passport photo, flight itinerary (round-trip or confirmed onward ticket), hotel bookings or a detailed travel itinerary for your entire stay, bank statements showing sufficient funds (usually last 3 months), copies of previous Chinese visas if any, and proof of legal residence if applying outside your home country. Additional documents may be required depending on your specific situation.

How long does a China tourist visa take to process?

Standard processing takes 4 to 5 business days from the date of submission at a Chinese Visa Application Service Center (CVASC) or embassy. Express service is available in most locations for an additional fee, reducing processing to 2 to 3 business days. Rush service can sometimes process in 1 business day but is not always available. Processing times vary by location and season.

How much does a Chinese tourist visa cost?

Fees depend on your nationality and the number of entries. For US citizens, the total is roughly $185 (including the visa fee plus the CVASC service fee). For UK citizens, around 150-170 GBP. For Canadian citizens, around 120-160 CAD. For Australian citizens, around 150-180 AUD. For most other nationalities, the fee ranges from $50-140 depending on reciprocity agreements.

Can I apply for a Chinese visa online or by mail?

In most countries, you must apply in person at a Chinese Visa Application Service Center. The application form is completed online through the COVA system, but you generally need to submit your passport and documents in person. Mail-in applications are not accepted. Some centers allow an agent or family member to submit on your behalf with a written authorization letter, but this varies by location.

What are the most common reasons for Chinese visa rejection?

Common rejection reasons include: incomplete or incorrect application form, insufficient funds shown in bank statements, missing flight or hotel bookings, a passport that is too close to expiring, applying too early (more than 3 months before travel), travel history that appears inconsistent, and providing documents that the officer finds insufficient to demonstrate you will return home after your trip.

Can I switch from a visa-free entry to a visa once inside China?

Generally no. If you enter China under a visa-free program, you cannot apply for or extend to a regular visa from inside the country. You must depart within the visa-free period. Visa extensions are only available to those who entered on a regular visa and have a legitimate reason to extend their stay.

Do children need their own visa?

Yes. Every traveler needs their own visa, regardless of age. Children must have their own passport and their own L visa. The application includes a copy of the child's birth certificate, copies of both parents' passports and visas, and a consent letter if only one parent is accompanying the child.

Is a Chinese visa valid for Hong Kong and Macau?

No. A Chinese mainland visa is not valid for entry into Hong Kong or Macau. These are Special Administrative Regions with separate visa policies. If you plan to visit Hong Kong or Macau during your trip, check their entry requirements separately.
