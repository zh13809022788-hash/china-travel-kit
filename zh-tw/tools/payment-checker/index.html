How to use this checker

  1. 1. Choose your passport or residence country. This helps frame common bank and verification issues.
  2. 2. Choose your card network. Visa and Mastercard are usually the best first choices for China travel wallets.
  3. 3. Prepare at least one fallback. Bring a second card, install both payment apps, and keep small cash for edge cases.

Payment rules, wallet verification, and issuer risk checks can change. Treat the result as practical trip planning guidance, then confirm any large transaction limits inside Alipay, WeChat Pay, and your card issuer before departure.

What this checker can and cannot tell you

This tool is designed for pre-departure planning. It helps you think through card network, wallet support, likely fee points, and backup methods before you are standing at a China checkout counter. It cannot guarantee that your individual bank will approve every payment.

Payment checker FAQ

Will every foreign Visa or Mastercard work in China?

No. Visa and Mastercard are the safest starting point for Alipay and WeChat Pay, but individual banks can still block verification, reject online wallet binding, or flag China transactions. Keep a second card and a small cash backup.

Should tourists set up both Alipay and WeChat Pay?

Yes, if possible. Alipay is often easier for visitors to set up, while WeChat Pay can be useful for mini programs, restaurants, and local services. Having both reduces the chance of being stuck at a merchant.

How much cash should I bring if mobile payment works?

Bring enough small notes for airport transport, backup meals, and small shops, but do not rely on cash alone. Many places prefer QR payment, and some staff may not have change ready.
