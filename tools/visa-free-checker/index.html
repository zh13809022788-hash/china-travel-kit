Policies change frequently and depend on your specific circumstances. Always confirm with a Chinese embassy or consulate before booking travel. This tool provides general guidance only.

Official sources to verify before booking

Visa-free country lists, entry ports, permitted travel areas, and airline boarding checks can change. Treat official government and airline rules as final.

  • National Immigration Administration for border inspection and transit exemption updates.
  • State Council information portal for English-language policy announcements.
  • Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Chinese embassy or consulate websites for passport-specific rules.

How to avoid visa-free mistakes

The most common error is mixing up ordinary visa-free entry with visa-free transit. A normal round trip and a stopover to a third country are not the same itinerary. Airlines check documents before boarding, so a misunderstanding can stop the trip before you reach China.

  • Check passport nationality first. Residence country and card billing country do not decide visa-free eligibility.
  • Separate entry from transit. Transit usually requires confirmed onward travel to a third country or region.
  • Confirm entry ports. Transit policies depend on designated ports and permitted travel areas.
  • Keep proof ready. Airlines and border officers may ask for onward ticket, hotel, itinerary, or invitation details.
