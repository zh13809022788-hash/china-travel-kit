You have downloaded Alipay, registered with your foreign phone number, and you are ready to link your credit card. Then the passport verification step stops you cold.

This is the most common frustration for foreign Alipay users, and it is almost always caused by one of five specific problems. Here is how to fix each one.

## Problem 1: Your Passport Photo Gets Rejected

This is the number one reason Alipay verification fails. The app asks you to take a photo of your passport photo page, and then tells you "verification failed" without explaining why.

**The fix:** Take the photo in bright but indirect light. Place your passport on a flat, dark-colored surface. Position your phone directly above the passport — not at an angle. Make sure all four corners of the photo page are visible. No fingers, no shadows, no glare.

If you are using an iPhone, turn off the flash and use the "Square" crop mode. If you are using an Android phone, enable "Document mode" if your camera app has one.

The machine-readable zone (the two lines of text, numbers, and chevrons at the bottom of the photo page) must be completely visible and not cut off. This is the part Alipay's system reads to extract your passport information.

**If it still fails:** Try scanning your passport using a different phone. Sometimes the camera quality or lighting conditions on a specific device cause persistent failures. Airport lounges often have good lighting and a flat surface for this.

## Problem 2: Your Name Does Not Match

Alipay requires your registered name to match your passport exactly. This includes middle names, suffixes (Jr., III), and the exact spelling of your first and last name.

**Where people get stuck:**
- You left out your middle name during registration — Alipay shows the name you entered, but the system compares it against the passport scan
- You used a nickname or a shortened version of your name — use your full legal name as shown on your passport
- Your passport uses a different character set (for example, a name with diacritical marks or non-Latin script) — register with the exact Latin-character version from your passport's machine-readable zone

**The fix:** If you already registered with the wrong name, you cannot change it yourself. You need to contact Alipay customer support through the app. Go to Help Center → Contact Us, explain that your name needs to be corrected, and provide a clear photo of your passport photo page.

## Problem 3: Your Credit Card Gets Rejected During Linking

Sometimes the passport verification succeeds, but the credit card linking step fails. This usually happens because of card-specific issues rather than Alipay problems.

**Cards that work:** Visa and Mastercard issued outside mainland China. UnionPay cards from any country. JCB cards from Japan. Discover cards.

**Cards that do not work:** American Express (not supported for foreign cards in Alipay). Prepaid cards that are not registered to your name. Virtual cards from services like Revolut or Wise that do not have your full name printed on them.

**The fix:** If your Visa or Mastercard is rejected, try a different card from a different bank. Some banks block Alipay transactions by default. Call your bank and ask them to "whitelist Alipay transactions" — they will know what this means.

If you do not have a second card, try registering through the Apple Pay option inside Alipay. This sometimes works when direct card linking does not, because Apple Pay handles the card authentication differently.

## Problem 4: Verification Takes Too Long

Automatic verification should complete within 5-10 minutes. If it has been longer than that, your application may have been flagged for manual review.

**How to check:** Open Alipay, go to Profile → Account Security → Identity Verification. If it still says "Pending," your application is in manual review. This can take 24-48 hours.

**What to do while waiting:** You can still use the app to browse and explore merchants, but you cannot make payments until verification completes. If you need to pay for something immediately, use cash or a different payment method.

**If it has been more than 48 hours:** Submit a new application with better quality photos. Sometimes the first application gets stuck in the system and a fresh submission moves faster.

## Problem 5: Your Passport Type Is Not Supported

Alipay's passport verification works with standard tourist passports from most countries. But there are edge cases:

- Diplomatic or official passports may not be recognized by the system
- Passports with non-standard formats (some older passport models, or passports with extended pages) may cause scanning issues
- Emergency passports or temporary travel documents are not supported

**The fix:** If your passport type is not supported, you have two options. Option one: ask a Chinese friend or your hotel front desk to send you a "red envelope" payment through Alipay — this sometimes bypasses the full verification. Option two: use WeChat Pay instead, which has a slightly different verification process that may work with your passport type.

## What to Do If Nothing Works

If you have tried all the fixes above and Alipay still does not verify your passport, you have three backup options:

1. **Use WeChat Pay** — the verification process is similar but sometimes succeeds where Alipay fails
2. **Use cash** — China is less cashless than most guidebooks suggest. ATMs at major banks accept foreign cards
3. **Get a Chinese bank card** — if you are staying long-term, open a bank account at Bank of China or ICBC. You need your passport, a Chinese phone number, and a visa with at least 90 days validity
