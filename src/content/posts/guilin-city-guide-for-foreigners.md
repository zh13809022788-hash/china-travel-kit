## Introduction

Guilin is the most scenic destination in China. There is no serious debate about this — the landscape of limestone karst peaks rising from the Li River has been celebrated in Chinese painting and poetry for over a thousand years, and when you see it in person you understand why. It is the image of China that exists in the Western imagination: mist-wrapped mountains, bamboo groves, water buffalo in rice paddies, and calm rivers with solitary fishermen on rafts.

For the foreign traveler, Guilin offers the iconic China landscape experience. It is also one of the easiest places in the country for independent travel. The tourism infrastructure in Guilin and especially in nearby Yangshuo is mature and foreigner-friendly. You can cycle through rice paddies with karst peaks on the horizon, take one of the world's great boat journeys, and eat well — all without much hassle.

## Getting Around

Guilin's airport (KWL) is about 30 km from the city. The airport shuttle bus takes about 40 minutes to the city center (20 yuan). Taxis cost 80-100 yuan. There is no metro, but the city is compact and taxis are cheap (flagfall 7 yuan).

Buses connect Guilin to Yangshuo (about 1.5 hours, 30 yuan) and to the Longji Rice Terraces (about 2.5 hours). High-speed trains connect Guilin to Guangzhou (2.5 hours), Guiyang (2 hours), and Kunming (4 hours).

In Yangshuo, bicycle is the primary mode of transport for sightseeing — rental is 20-40 yuan per day. Electric scooters are also available (60-80 yuan per day). Taxis and ride-hailing are available but limited compared to major cities.

## Top Attractions

### Li River Cruise (漓江游船)

The four-hour cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo is the essential Guilin experience. The boats depart from Zhujiang Pier in the morning (around 9 AM) and arrive in Yangshuo by early afternoon. The scenery changes continuously — each bend in the river reveals another composition of karst peaks, bamboo groves, and riverside villages. The stretch between Xingping and Yangshuo is the most photographed section and appears on the 20 yuan banknote.

Book through your hotel or a reputable travel agency. Standard boats cost 400-500 yuan including lunch. VIP boats with better seating are 600-700 yuan.

### Yangshuo County (阳朔)

Yangshuo is the real destination for most travelers — a small town surrounded by the most dramatic karst scenery in the region. The main street (West Street, 西街) is famously touristy but still fun for an evening stroll. The real magic is the countryside: rent a bicycle and ride out to Moon Hill, take a bamboo raft on the Yulong River, or hike to the top of Xianggong Mountain for sunrise views over the Li River.

Yangshuo is also China's rock climbing capital, with hundreds of routes on the karst cliffs suitable for beginners and experts alike.

### Reed Flute Cave (芦笛岩)

A spectacular limestone cave filled with stalactites and stalagmites, illuminated with colored lights. It is very much a tourist attraction (the lighting is dramatic), but the scale of the cave is genuinely impressive. Located just outside Guilin city. Entry is 110 yuan. Allow 1.5 hours.

### Elephant Trunk Hill (象鼻山)

Guilin's symbol — a hill shaped like an elephant drinking from the river. It is a quick stop (30-45 minutes) and more of a photo opportunity than a full attraction, but it is free and located right in the city. Go early to avoid the crowds.

### Longji Rice Terraces (龙脊梯田)

Also known as the Dragon's Backbone Rice Terraces, this is a stunning landscape of terraced rice fields carved into the mountainside by Zhuang and Yao minority communities over centuries. The terraces are at their most spectacular in April-June when they are flooded with water and reflect the sky, and in September-October when the rice turns golden before harvest.

The site is about 2.5 hours from Guilin. You can take a bus from Guilin or join a day tour. Entry is 80 yuan. If you have time, stay overnight in a minority village guesthouse for the sunrise.

## Where to Stay

**Guilin city center (near Zhengyang Pedestrian Street)** — Most convenient for arriving and departing. Good hotel options, easy access to the Li River cruise pier and airport shuttle.

**Yangshuo (West Street area)** — More atmospheric and scenic. Better for longer stays. Hotels range from budget hostels to luxury resorts. Book in advance during peak season.

**Longji Rice Terraces** — Minority village guesthouses. Basic but unforgettable. Best for those who want to experience the sunrise over the terraces.

## Food

Guilin's cuisine reflects its Guangxi location — lighter than northern Chinese food, with influences from the neighboring Zhuang and Yao minority cultures.

**Guilin rice noodles (桂林米粉)** is the city's signature dish and one of China's great breakfasts. Rice noodles in a bone broth with preserved vegetables, peanuts, chili, and your choice of meat. It costs 6-15 yuan and is sold everywhere — eat it for breakfast like the locals do.

**Beer fish (啤酒鱼)** is the specialty of Yangshuo. Fresh river fish cooked in a wok with beer, tomatoes, and peppers. It is served in a hot pot and is excellent. The best beer fish is found in Yangshuo, not Guilin.

**Stuffed li (酿菜)** — various vegetables (peppers, eggplants, bitter gourds) stuffed with minced pork and steamed. A Guangxi specialty.

## Practical Tips

- **Getting to Yangshuo:** The bus from Guilin takes about 1.5 hours (30 yuan). There are also direct buses from the airport. High-speed trains now stop at Yangshuo Station (about 30 minutes from Guilin).
- **Language:** Guilin and Yangshuo are more foreigner-friendly than most Chinese cities. English menus are common in Yangshuo.
- **Weather:** Guilin is humid year-round. Pack an umbrella even in the dry season. The best months for photography are April-June (flooded rice terraces) and September-October (golden rice and clear skies).
- **Visa:** 240-hour visa-free transit is available for eligible nationalities through Guangxi entry ports.
- **The 20 yuan note:** Bring a 20 yuan bill from your home country — the scene on the back is the Li River landscape near Xingping. You can take the exact same photo.
- **Don't overplan:** The best Guilin experiences are spontaneous — cycling through Yangshuo countryside, stopping at a riverside cafe, watching farmers work the rice paddies. Leave room for serendipity.
