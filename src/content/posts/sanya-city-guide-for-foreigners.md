## Introduction

Sanya sits at the southern tip of Hainan, China's tropical island province, and it is the country's premier beach destination. While the rest of China shivers through winter, Sanya stays warm and sunny, which is why it fills with domestic tourists — and a growing number of foreigners — from November through spring. Palm-lined bays, seafood, island excursions, and a resort atmosphere make it a change of pace from urban China. This guide covers what a foreign visitor or long-stay winter resident needs to know.

## Best Season

Timing is everything in Sanya. **Winter to spring (roughly November-April)** is the sweet spot: warm, dry, and sunny, ideal for the beach. **Summer (June-September)** is hot, humid, and the rainy season, with the possibility of typhoons. If you want reliable beach weather, plan for the cooler half of the year; if you come in summer, expect heat and afternoon downpours.

## Getting Around

Sanya is spread along its bays, so getting between areas matters. The city has buses and a compact rail presence, but most visitors rely on DiDi ride-hailing (which works with a foreign card) and taxis to move between Sanya Bay, Dadonghai, and Yalong Bay. Keep small cash for older taxis. Within a single bay area, walking and shared bikes cover short distances.

## Beaches & Areas

- **Dadonghai:** compact, walkable, good swimming and nightlife — the easiest base for first-timers.
- **Yalong Bay:** the upscale resort strip with the finest sand and calmest water.
- **Sanya Bay:** long, central, and better value, with a more local feel.
- **Wuzhizhou Island:** a popular day trip for clear water and snorkeling.

## Money & Connectivity

As across China, set up mobile payment before you arrive — Alipay or WeChat Pay linked to a foreign card covers almost all spending by QR code. Get an eSIM so you are connected on arrival, and keep some small cash for taxis and small vendors. Resorts may accept foreign cards directly, but do not rely on it elsewhere.

## Food

Seafood is Sanya's specialty — pick fresh catch at a seafood market or restaurant and have it cooked to order. Hainanese specialties like Wenchang chicken and tropical fruits round out the local table. Resort areas offer international options, and delivery apps with in-app translation make ordering easy.

## Summary

Sanya offers a tropical beach escape unlike anywhere else in China, at its best in the winter months when the weather is warm and dry. For a short trip it is a relaxing change from the mainland's cities; for a long winter stay it is a comfortable, affordable seasonal base — just plan around the summer heat and rains.
