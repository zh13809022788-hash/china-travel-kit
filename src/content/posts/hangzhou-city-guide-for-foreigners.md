## Introduction

Hangzhou is the scenic counterpoint to nearby Shanghai — a city that has been celebrated for its beauty for over a thousand years, centered on the willow-lined causeways and pagodas of West Lake. Today it is also one of China's tech and e-commerce capitals, giving it a modern, prosperous, software-driven character alongside its classical scenery. With high-speed trains connecting it to Shanghai in under an hour, it works equally well as a day trip, a relaxed few-day stay, or a longer base. This guide covers the essentials for foreign visitors.

## Getting Around

Hangzhou's metro is modern, cheap, and bilingual, and it reaches West Lake and the main districts. Add the transit QR code to Alipay or WeChat Pay, or use English-language ticket machines. Around West Lake itself, walking and cycling are the best ways to explore — shared bikes are everywhere and the lakeside paths are flat and pleasant.

DiDi ride-hailing works with a foreign card for trips out to the tea villages and hills. Metered taxis are available; keep small cash for older cabs.

## Top Sights

- **West Lake:** the heart of any visit — walk or cycle the Su and Bai causeways, see the pagodas, and take a boat to the islands.
- **Longjing tea villages:** the hills west of the lake produce China's famous Dragon Well green tea; visit a village teahouse at the source.
- **Lingyin Temple:** a large, atmospheric Buddhist temple complex with carved grottoes, set in forested hills.
- **Grand Canal & Xiaohe Street:** the historic canal district with restored old streets and waterside life.
- **Hefang Street:** a lively old commercial street for snacks and souvenirs.

## Money & Connectivity

As across China, mobile payment is essential — set up Alipay or WeChat Pay with a foreign card before arrival and pay by QR nearly everywhere. Get an eSIM for connectivity on landing, and keep some small cash. Direct foreign-card acceptance is limited to international hotels and upscale malls.

## Food

Hangzhou cuisine is milder and subtler than Sichuan's, known for dishes like West Lake vinegar fish, Dongpo pork, and beggar's chicken, plus fresh seasonal ingredients and, of course, Longjing tea used in cooking. Lakeside restaurants and the old streets offer plenty of approachable options, with picture menus and delivery apps easing ordering.

## Summary

Hangzhou blends timeless scenery with a modern tech economy and a gentler pace than its giant neighbor. For short-trip visitors it is one of the most beautiful and accessible cities in eastern China; for remote workers and longer-stay residents it offers an appealing mix of infrastructure, livability, and lower costs.
