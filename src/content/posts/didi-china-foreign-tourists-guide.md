## Introduction

Hailing a taxi in an unfamiliar Chinese city is one of the most stressful moments of any trip. You stand on a curb, empty cabs sail past, and when one finally stops you face a driver who speaks no English and a destination you cannot pronounce. Street taxis may not take cards, meters occasionally take detours, and explaining where you want to go can dissolve into pointing and hopeful nodding.

Didi solves nearly all of this. As China's dominant ride-hailing platform, it does the talking for you: your destination is entered in the app, the fare is fixed before you ride, and payment happens automatically from your phone. Best of all for visitors, Didi now works comfortably in English and accepts foreign payment methods. This guide walks you through getting the app, setting up payment, booking your first ride, and handling the language and safety details so your first trip goes smoothly.

## Before You Begin

A little preparation at home, while you still have stable internet, saves a lot of fumbling on arrival.

Here is what you need:

- A smartphone with room for one or two apps and a live data connection in China (a travel eSIM is the easiest option).
- A mobile number that can receive an SMS verification code. Your home-country number works fine.
- A supported international card (Visa, Mastercard, or JCB), ideally already linked to Alipay or WeChat before you travel.
- Your passport, which you may need for the identity check when linking a foreign card to a wallet.

There are three ways to access Didi, and choosing the right one matters:

- **The standalone DiDi app.** A full English interface with English in-app support. Best if you want a dedicated ride-hailing app and plan to use it often.
- **The Didi mini-program inside Alipay.** No separate download. If you already use Alipay for payments, this is usually the smoothest path because your linked foreign card is ready to go.
- **The Didi mini-program inside WeChat.** Similar to the Alipay route, useful if WeChat is your main wallet in China.

For most short-stay tourists, the Alipay mini-program is the path of least resistance: you set up one wallet, and both payments and rides run through it. If you travel to China regularly or want the richest features, install the standalone app as well.

## Step-by-Step: Booking Your First Didi

### 1. Get Didi

If you prefer the standalone app, download DiDi from the App Store or Google Play (or your Android phone's app store) and open it. Choose English as your language if prompted.

If you prefer the mini-program, open Alipay, make sure the app language is set to English under settings, then search for Didi in the Alipay search bar and open the mini-program. The WeChat route works the same way through WeChat's search.

### 2. Register your account

Enter your mobile number and the country code for your home country. Didi sends an SMS verification code; type it in to create your account. If you are using the Alipay or WeChat mini-program, you may be logged in automatically through your existing wallet account, skipping this step entirely.

### 3. Set up payment

This is where the wallet approach shines.

If you are booking inside the Alipay or WeChat mini-program, Didi charges the international card already linked to that wallet, so there is nothing more to do. If you have not linked a card yet, open your wallet's card section, add your Visa, Mastercard, or JCB, and complete the one-time passport identity check first.

In the standalone DiDi app, open the payment settings and add a card or connect a wallet. Foreign card support in the standalone app has improved but can be inconsistent, so if a card is refused, switch to booking through Alipay instead.

### 4. Set your pickup point

Didi uses your phone's GPS to place a pickup pin automatically. Check it carefully, since GPS can land you across a wide road or on the wrong side of a large building or mall. Drag the map pin to the exact spot where you are standing, or type a nearby landmark in English. Naming a clear landmark (a hotel, metro exit, or well-known building) makes it far easier for the driver to find you.

### 5. Enter your destination

Tap the destination field and type in English. Didi's search recognizes English names for hotels, airports, attractions, malls, and metro stations, and shows matching results on the map. Select the correct one and confirm the pin location. If an obscure address will not resolve in English, drop the pin manually on the map or search for the nearest major landmark and adjust.

### 6. Choose your ride type

Didi shows several tiers, each with an upfront estimated fare:

- **Express** — the standard, economical everyday car. The best default for most trips.
- **Taxi** — hails a licensed metered taxi through the app.
- **Premier / Comfort / Luxe** — newer, larger, or higher-end cars at higher prices.

Pick the tier that fits your budget and group size, then review the estimated fare shown before you commit.

### 7. Confirm and ride

Tap to book. The app matches you with a driver and displays their name, photo, car model, and license plate number, plus an estimated arrival time. When the car pulls up, confirm that the plate and the driver match what the app shows before you get in. You do not need to state your destination; the driver already has it.

### 8. Pay, tip, and rate

When the ride ends, payment is deducted automatically from your linked card or wallet, so there is no cash and no card machine to fuss with. Tipping is not expected in China, though the app may offer the option. Finally, rate your driver with a star score; honest ratings keep the service reliable for everyone.

## Language, Safety, and Etiquette

The single biggest advantage of Didi is that it removes the conversation. Your route is on the driver's screen, so a smooth trip needs almost no talking. When you do need to communicate (a change of pickup spot, a quick question), use the in-app messaging, which translates your English into Chinese for the driver and their replies back into English for you. The in-app call feature routes through a masked number that protects your real phone number.

On safety, Didi includes several tools worth using:

- **Trip sharing** — send your live route and driver details to an emergency contact, ideal for late-night or long rides.
- **Emergency SOS** — an in-app button that alerts Didi's safety center and can contact authorities.
- **Trip recording and GPS tracking** — the route is logged, which deters detours.
- **Driver verification** — drivers are registered with photo and plate details you can check against the car.

Simple etiquette helps: sit in the back seat unless traveling in a group, keep the doors closed until the car has fully stopped, and cancel promptly if your plans change so the driver is not left waiting. Buckle up; seatbelt use is expected.

## Costs and Troubleshooting

Didi fares are calculated from a base fare plus distance and time, and the estimate you see before booking is usually close to the final charge. Express rides are typically comparable to or a little cheaper than a street taxi. Prices climb with **surge pricing** during morning and evening rush hours, in rain, and around public holidays, when demand outstrips available cars. If a fare looks high, waiting ten minutes or switching to the Taxi tier can bring it down.

A few common snags and how to handle them:

- **No drivers available.** Switch ride types or wait a few minutes. Express and Taxi tiers usually have the most cars.
- **Payment declined.** Confirm your card is linked and verified in your wallet, and that you are within its limits. Booking through Alipay is the most reliable payment route for foreign cards.
- **Destination will not resolve in English.** Drop the map pin manually, or search for a large nearby landmark and adjust from there.
- **Driver cannot find you.** Message them your exact spot through the app, or move to a clearly named landmark such as a hotel entrance or metro exit.
- **App is in Chinese.** In the standalone app, change the language in settings; in a mini-program, set your Alipay or WeChat app language to English.

For anything the app cannot solve, Didi offers English-language in-app support through the help section, where you can report issues, recover lost items, or dispute a fare.

## Summary

Didi turns one of the hardest parts of travel in China into one of the easiest. By booking through the standalone DiDi app or, more simply, the Didi mini-program inside Alipay or WeChat, foreign tourists get an English interface, upfront fares, and automatic payment from a linked international card. Enter your pickup and destination in English, check the map pins, pick a ride tier, and confirm the plate before you climb in. Lean on the in-app translation for any communication, use the trip-sharing and SOS safety features on longer rides, and remember that fares rise during surge periods. Set up your wallet and app before you land, and you will step off the plane ready to get anywhere in the city without speaking a word of Chinese.
