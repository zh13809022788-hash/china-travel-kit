## Introduction

Xi'an is where Chinese civilization crystallized. It was the capital of 13 dynasties spanning over 1,100 years — the most in Chinese history — and served as the eastern terminus of the Silk Road. The Terracotta Warriors alone would make it a world-class destination, but Xi'an offers so much more: one of the best-preserved city walls in the world, a Muslim quarter that dates back to the 7th century, and a food scene that rivals any in China.

For the foreign traveler in 2026, Xi'an is essential. It is the city that connects the imperial grandeur of Beijing to the ancient trade routes of Central Asia. You come here for history, but you stay for the food, the relaxed pace, and the feeling of walking through layers of time that few cities on earth can match.

## Getting Around

The Xi'an Metro has grown rapidly and now covers all major attractions. Line 2 runs north-south through the city center and is the most useful for tourists. Line 3 and Line 4 connect to the railway stations and the east side. Stations have bilingual signage, and mobile payment via Alipay QR code works at the gates.

The City Wall is 14 kilometers around and you can rent a bicycle to cycle the entire perimeter — one of the great urban experiences in China. E-bikes are also available. It takes about 1.5-2 hours to cycle the full circuit.

DiDi is reliable in Xi'an. Taxis are very cheap (flagfall is about 8 yuan) but expect language barriers. For the Terracotta Warriors, buses run from the Xi'an Railway Station, or you can hire a driver for the day (about 300-400 yuan).

## Top Attractions

### Terracotta Warriors (兵马俑)

The discovery that reshaped world archaeology. In 1974, local farmers digging a well stumbled upon the life-sized army of Qin Shi Huang, China's first emperor. Over 2,000 warriors have been excavated across three pits, each soldier individually crafted with distinct facial features. Pit 1 is the showstopper — row after row of infantry, archers, and charioteers standing in battle formation, each face unique.

The museum complex is well-organized. Go immediately when it opens. The first hour is peaceful; after 10 AM the tour groups arrive in waves. Allow 2-3 hours on site. The site is 40 km east of the city. Get there by bus from the Xi'an Railway Station (Line 5, 306 bus, about 1 hour) or by taxi/DiDi (about 150 yuan one way, 45 minutes).

### Xi'an City Wall (西安城墙)

The most complete ancient city wall in China, and one of the best-preserved in the world. Built in the Ming Dynasty on the foundations of the Tang imperial wall, its 14-kilometer perimeter encloses the old city. The wall is wide enough for vehicles — you can walk, cycle, or take an electric cart along the top. Cycling the full circuit is the quintessential Xi'an experience, especially at sunset when the old city glows golden and the modern skyline lights up beyond.

Rent a bicycle at the South Gate (永宁门) — the most impressive gate and the best place to start. Entry is 54 yuan. Bicycle rental is about 45 yuan. Allow 2 hours for cycling or 3-4 hours for walking.

### Muslim Quarter and Great Mosque (回民街·大清真寺)

Xi'an's Muslim Quarter has been home to Chinese Muslims (the Hui people) for over 1,300 years, since the Silk Road brought traders and settlers from Central Asia and Persia. The Great Mosque is a remarkable fusion of Chinese architectural forms and Islamic function — pagoda-like minarets, courtyard gardens, and Arabic calligraphy on Chinese screens.

The surrounding streets form one of China's best food streets. It is crowded, noisy, and wonderful. Go in the evening for the full atmosphere, but arrive early (around 5-6 PM) before it gets uncomfortably packed. The mosque is 25 yuan entry. Walking the quarter is free.

### Shaanxi History Museum (陕西历史博物馆)

One of China's greatest museums, often ranked alongside the Beijing Palace Museum and Nanjing Museum. The collection covers over a million years of history, from prehistoric tools through the Zhou, Qin, Han, Tang, and Ming dynasties. The Tang dynasty collection is the highlight — the cosmopolitan art of China's golden age, when Xi'an (then Chang'an) was the world's most sophisticated city.

Entry is free but requires advance reservation on the WeChat mini-program. Reservations open 14 days ahead and fill up fast, especially on weekends. Book early. The reserve ticket lets you skip much of the queue. Allow 3-4 hours.

### Big Wild Goose Pagoda (大雁塔)

The iconic pagoda of Xi'an, built in 652 AD during the Tang Dynasty to house Buddhist scriptures brought from India by the monk Xuanzang. The surrounding plaza has a large fountain show in the evenings. You can climb the pagoda for views over the city (25 yuan). The nearby Tang Paradise (大唐芙蓉园) is a reconstructed Tang Dynasty palace garden — beautiful but expensive (120 yuan) and more of a theme park experience.

## Where to Stay

**City Wall area (South Gate / Yongningmen)** — The best area for tourists. Historic, walkable, excellent food nearby, and close to the metro. Hotels range from budget to mid-range.

**Muslim Quarter** — The most atmospheric area but can be noisy at night. Best for food lovers and those who want to be in the thick of things.

**Xi'an North Station area** — Convenient for the airport express and high-speed rail. Modern but characterless. Good if you're just passing through or have an early train.

## Food

Xi'an's food is rugged, hearty, and wheat-based — a northern Chinese cuisine shaped by Central Asian and Muslim influences.

**Yangrou paomo (羊肉泡馍)** is the city's most famous dish. You are given a bowl of hard flatbread to crumble into tiny pieces (the smaller the pieces, the more respect you show the chef), then it is taken away and returned as a rich lamb soup. It is heavy, warming, and deeply satisfying.

**Biang biang noodles** are hand-pulled until they are thick, wide, and chewy, then tossed in chili oil, vinegar, and garlic. The name comes from the sound of the dough slapping the counter.

**Roujiamo (肉夹馍)** is often called the "Chinese hamburger" — braised pork belly chopped fine and stuffed into a freshly baked flatbread. It costs about 8-12 yuan and is the best cheap eat in the city.

**Liangpi (凉皮)** are cold rice-noodle sheets served with chili oil, vinegar, and bean sprouts. Perfect for hot days.

The best place to eat is the Muslim Quarter, specifically the side streets off the main drag and the area around Dapiyuan (大皮院).

## Practical Tips

- **Terracotta Warriors tickets:** Buy online in advance through official channels (the museum's WeChat mini-program or travel platforms). Do not buy from touts who approach you on the street — they charge inflated prices for transport packages that include fake or low-quality side attractions.
- **Getting to the Warriors:** The most reliable way is the public bus (Line 306, also called the Tourist Bus 5) from the Xi'an Railway Station. It costs about 8 yuan and leaves when full. Ignore anyone who tries to redirect you to a different bus — there are many fake tourist buses targeting foreigners near the station.
- **Payments:** Alipay and WeChat Pay with a foreign card work at most restaurants, hotels, and attractions. Carry 200-500 yuan cash for small food stalls in the Muslim Quarter.
- **Language:** Major sites have English. Muslim Quarter vendors are used to foreigners and will communicate with hand gestures and calculators for prices. Download Pleco for text translation and Google Translate or DeepL for voice.
- **Best season:** Autumn (Sep-Nov) is perfect. Spring (Mar-May) is good but dusty. Summer is brutally hot. Winter is cold but uncrowded.
- **Visa:** Xi'an operates the 240-hour visa-free transit policy for eligible nationalities, provided you arrive and depart through Shaanxi entry ports.
